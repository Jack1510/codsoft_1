First Year Exam

The first-year exam pattern at engineering, like most engineering colleges, usually involves two main types of exams:

  1. Internal Assessments/Tests: These are conducted by the college throughout the academic year. They can be in the form of unit tests, quizzes, assignments, or class assessments. The purpose of these exams is to evaluate your progress and understanding of the subjects taught during each semester.
  2. Semester Examinations: At the end of each semester, there are comprehensive exams that cover all the topics taught during that particular semester. These exams are conducted by the university to assess your overall performance and knowledge gained throughout the semester.

Other aspects of the exam pattern might include:

  • The weightage of marks for internal assessments and semester exams may vary depending on the course and the subject.
  • The exams may consist of a combination of theoretical questions, practical problems, and objective-type questions.
  • Some courses might have a project component that contributes to the overall assessment.
  • In some cases, viva-voce (oral examinations) may be conducted for specific subjects or projects.

Second Year Exam

The second-year exam pattern of any engineering college, typically includes the following components:

  1. Internal Assessments: Similar to the first year, internal assessments are likely to continue in the second year as well. These could include unit tests, quizzes, assignments, laboratory assessments, and periodic evaluations conducted throughout the semester.
  2. Semester Examinations: At the end of each semester, comprehensive exams are conducted to assess the students' understanding and knowledge of the subjects taught during that semester
  3. Practical Examinations: Practical exams are conducted for subjects that involve hands-on learning, such as laboratory courses. Students might be required to perform experiments or demonstrate their skills during these practical exams.
  4. Minor Projects and Presentations: In the second year, there might be projects assigned to students, either individually or in groups, depending on the course and curriculum. The projects could involve research, design, or implementation tasks. Students may also be required to present their project work.
  5. Viva-Voce: Viva-voce or oral examinations may be conducted for specific subjects or projects. During viva-voce, students might be questioned on their understanding of the subject matter, project work, or other related topics.
  6. Weightage of Marks: The weightage of marks for each component, i.e., internal assessments, semester exams, practical exams, and projects, can vary from subject to subject and across different courses.
